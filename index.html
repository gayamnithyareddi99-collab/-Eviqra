How It Works

From Retail Incident to Police-Ready Evidence Pack in Minutes

Every Eviqra deployment follows a proven four-stage evidence capture journey from incident logging and guided CCTV documentation, to witness statement orchestration and DEMS-compliant pack generation all without requiring legal expertise from your frontline retail staff.

STEP 01

Log the Incident

Staff complete a guided, retail-specific form. Auto-generated chronological timelines capture approach, theft, confrontation, and exit with precision and consistency.

STEP 02

Capture CCTV Evidence

Eviqra corrects DVR timestamp offsets and prompts "best stills" selection using a scored, repeatable protocol that meets police identification-grade image standards.

STEP 03

Orchestrate Statements

Dual-statement orchestration links staff witness accounts and CCTV operator logs to specific exhibit IDs in one structured workflow eliminating manual errors.

STEP 04

Submit & Track

Generate a DEMS-ready evidence bundle with machine-readable manifests. Cryptographic hashing confirms integrity before police submission. Track case outcomes and close evidence gaps.

Core Capabilities

Five Engines. One Evidence Intelligence Layer.

Eviqra combines five proprietary workflow modules into a single police-ready evidence operating system the only platform that unifies incident capture, evidence rules enforcement, video time normalisation, statement orchestration, and outcome-linked analytics into one prosecution-ready infrastructure for UK retailers.

Engine 01

Evidence Rules Engine

Dynamically enforces minimum required evidence per incident type. Export is blocked until all standards are met or waived with documented rationale no incomplete submissions.

Unlike static checklists, this enforces compliance at capture time. Incident types include shoplifting, assault, threats, criminal damage, and fuel drive-off. Each type carries its own required evidence thresholds, ensuring every pack reaching the police is truly complete.
Engine 02

DEMS-Ready Export & Manifest

Generates structured, machine-readable police intake packages with exhibit lists, timestamps, camera IDs, and continuity notes aligned with UK police DEMS intake specifications.

Retailer-side structured export for direct police intake is rarely implemented by existing platforms. Eviqra's manifest format reduces friction in evidence submission, accelerates case progression, and eliminates the rework that kills cases before prosecution.
Engine 03

Video Time Normalisation

Automatically adjusts CCTV clock drift and DVR offsets, producing a corrected "police-time" timeline while preserving original timestamps for audit integrity.

Operationalises a known failure mode (DVR time drift) into an automated workflow. Accurate incident timelines are critical for investigation and prosecution. Automated overwrite countdown triggers site-specific alerts before footage is lost forever.
Engine 04

Dual-Statement Orchestration

Staff witness and CCTV operator statements generated in one structured flow, automatically linked to exhibit IDs combining legal narrative creation with evidence continuity.

Standard witness statement templates guide staff through every key evidential point. Automated prosecution-friendly narrative generation creates indexed incident packs with built-in disclosure hygiene at the retailer level not just post-submission.
Engine 05

Outcome-Linked Analytics

Correlates missing evidence elements to case progression outcomes a data-driven feedback loop that improves evidence quality over time and maximises retailer ROI.

Competitors offer reporting dashboards but not quality-to-outcome modelling. Eviqra tracks which evidence gaps correlate with non-progression, enabling retailers to learn and continuously strengthen their incident documentation practices.
Engine 06

Forecourt Drive-Off Compiler

Purpose-built for UK forecourts: integrates pump transaction data, ANPR vehicle reads, and CCTV video into a single indexed evidential bundle for each drive-off incident.

Specialist use-case not addressed in general reporting systems. Links transaction data to vehicle identification and video evidence seamlessly, increasing prosecution potential for forecourt thefts in one structured cross-referenced pack.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Eviqra integrate with our existing CCTV systems?

+
Eviqra works with all standard CCTV systems. Staff export footage using your existing DVR/NVR system, then upload clips through Eviqra's evidence portal. The platform's video time normalisation automatically corrects timestamp offsets and generates police-compliant timelines without requiring any direct DVR integration.

What makes Eviqra evidence packs different from manual reporting?

+
Manual reporting produces inconsistent, incomplete packs that police frequently cannot act on. Eviqra's minimum viable evidence rules engine enforces required evidence at capture time blocking submission until standards are met. Every pack includes cryptographic hash verification, DEMS-compliant manifests, normalised timelines, and dual-statement linkage that manual processes cannot replicate.

How long does it take to train staff to use Eviqra?

+
Most frontline staff are proficient within 30–60 minutes of guided onboarding. Eviqra's forms are designed for non-technical retail workers step-by-step prompts guide staff through exactly what evidence to capture without requiring any legal or technical knowledge. Our pilot data shows 80%+ staff adoption within the first week of deployment.

Is Eviqra compliant with UK GDPR and evidence handling regulations?

+
Yes. Eviqra is built with UK GDPR compliance by design, incorporating data minimisation, role-based access controls, and audit-trail requirements. Evidence data is stored within UK borders. The platform is designed around UK police evidence management standards (DEMS), Home Office CCTV handling guidance, and digital evidence integrity requirements for UK criminal proceedings.

Can Eviqra handle multi-site retailers with different regional crime patterns?

+
Absolutely. Eviqra's multi-site analytics dashboards allow Loss Prevention Managers to compare evidence completeness, incident frequency, and case progression across all locations. Site-specific CCTV overwrite countdowns account for different DVR retention periods per location, and regional crime analytics help prioritise resource deployment to highest-risk areas.
